Why i started photography?

In my family, both my parents work as photographers, so I was already around cameras at a young age. My mother is an Architecture/Publicity photographer whereas my father, after starting his career as a War photographer, is now a Wildlife photographer. Since my mother's studio is located at home, I had the chance to be exposed often to her working environment. On weekends I used to go hiking in Lebanon's mountains with my father while he was shooting photos for his book "The other Lebanon ". You can find a link to both of their websites in the " Parents section ".

I didn't start taking pictures right away. I was more interested in filming the surf trips I used to go on with my dad. That's how I decided to buy a GoPro camera and to produce two short movies entitled "Surf Tripin'" available "here".

After that period, I enjoyed taking pictures with my iPhone but I quickly realized that I shot too many pictures of the same things and that these pictures ended up in my iPhone library, completely forgotten. This is why, last summer, I decided to buy my first camera, a Canon AE-1 Program, which is a film camera. The film camera is the ancestor of the digital camera: it only works with films that usually contain 24 or 36 pictures. I felt that working with film would challenge me and push me to focus more time on making each single shot. And the exciting thing about it is that you can only see the result of the photo after you develop the films, so you always have surprises.
